## Finding 2: E-Prime compliance is partial and asymmetric
The model struggles most with 'are' (plural copula) — 31 of 52 violations.
'Is' (singular copula) was fully avoided, suggesting the model treats
'are' as less salient to the E-Prime constraint than 'is'.
No-Have compliance was near-perfect (97.1%), suggesting 'to have' is
easier to route around than 'to be' — itself a finding about the
centrality of the copula in English reasoning patterns.

## Finding 3: E-Prime disrupts answer framing
2 of 15 causal reasoning items had extraction failures under E-Prime
(vs 0 for control, 1 for no-have). The model cannot say 'the answer is B'
and must find alternative framings, which don't always match standard patterns.
Practical implication: evaluation pipelines using constrained prompting need
robust answer extraction that doesn't assume 'X is Y' framing.
